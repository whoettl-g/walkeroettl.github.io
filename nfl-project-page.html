The NFL's Dwindling Middle Class

NFLPA Analytics Competition | Fall 2024 | Team of 4 (Finalist)

R Excel K-Means Clustering Policy Analysis

Overview

The NFL's salary cap creates a zero-sum environment where every dollar spent on one player is a dollar unavailable for another. Our team investigated a growing concern: the middle class of NFL veterans—players earning between $1.9M and $6.3M annually—is shrinking as star players command an ever-larger share of team payrolls.

We analyzed 14 years of contract data (2011–2024) from Spotrac, Over-the-Cap, and Pro Football Reference, standardizing all figures to the 2024 salary cap of $255 million. Using K-means clustering, we identified three distinct economic classes and tracked their evolution over time.

My Role

On a team of four, I led the policy modeling—designing the Mid-Level Exception and Team-Level Exception proposals, including the progressive tax funding mechanism. I also led our final presentation to NFLPA executives, including the union president, where we defended our methodology and recommendations.

Key Findings

The middle class salary range has contracted from $5.3M to $4.8M over the study period. More critically, the middle class's share of dead money (guaranteed money paid to released players) dropped from 28% to 19% after the 2020 CBA, while the upper class's share grew from 64% to 75%. This shift indicates that guaranteed money—and therefore job security—is increasingly concentrated among elite players.

Policy Recommendations

We proposed two mechanisms to incentivize teams to invest in middle-class talent:

Mid-Level Exception: Modeled after the NBA, this would provide teams a $750,000 cap credit for signing qualifying veterans to one-year, fully-guaranteed contracts at position-specific rates. Our modeling showed an average player salary increase of $415,000 with team cap savings of $335,000 per contract.

Team-Level Exception: A roster-composition incentive that rewards teams based on how many middle-class players they employ across position groups, funded through a progressive tax on the Performance-Based Pay pool.
